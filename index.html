Full Grammar Book

1. Noun

A noun is the name of a person, place, animal, thing, or idea.

Examples: Ali, Karachi, Cat, Book, Love

2. Pronoun

A pronoun is used instead of a noun.

Examples: He, She, It, They, We

3. Verb

A verb shows action or state.

Examples: Run, Eat, Sleep, Is, Are

4. Adjective

An adjective describes a noun.

Examples: Beautiful girl, Big house

5. Adverb

An adverb describes a verb, adjective, or another adverb.

Examples: Slowly, Quickly, Very

6. Preposition

Shows relation of place/time.

Examples: in, on, at, under, behind

7. Conjunction

Joins words or sentences.

Examples: and, but, because

8. Interjection

Expresses sudden feeling.

Examples: Wow! Oh! Alas!

9. Articles

A, An, The

10. Singular / Plural

Book → Books, Child → Children

11. Tenses

Present, Past, Future with 12 forms.

12. Active / Passive

Ali wrote a letter. / A letter was written by Ali.

13. Direct / Indirect

He said, “I am happy.” / He said that he was happy.

14. Modals

can, could, may, might, should, must

15. Conditionals

If you study, you will pass.

16. Subject Verb Agreement

He goes. They go.

17. Common Errors

Wrong: He go school.

Correct: He goes to school.
